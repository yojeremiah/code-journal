Bodybuilding Career

Arnold "The Austrian Oak" Schwarzenegger began lifting weights at the age of 15; at age 20 he won the title of Mr. Universe and would go on to win the Mr. Olympia contest seven times throughout his professional weightlifting career. He is widely considered to be one of the greatest bodybuilders of all time and is known as one of the sport's most charismatic ambassadors.

Professional Acting

Schwarzenegger transitioned from the gymnasium to the big-screen, landing a debut professional role as Hercules in 1969. He would go on to star in numerous action-packed films including "Conan the Barbarian", "Predator" and (arguably his most notable title) "The Terminator". The renowned actor's captivating skills landed him a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 1987.

Political Endeavors

Arnold was appointed as chairman of the President's Council on Physical Fitness and Sports, on which he served from 1990 to 1993. He would go on to win a seat as the Governor of California in a 2003 special election. Re-running as an incumbent, he would win the seat again in 2006. Arnold served as the Governor of California until 2011 and became known as the "Governator".
